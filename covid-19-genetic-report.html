COVID-19 Combined Genetics and Lifestyle Report

The COVID-19 Combined Genetics and Lifestyle report is a useful tool that combines the power of genetics and lifestyle to assess your risk of acquiring the coronavirus infection and your severity of the disease. It includes assessing your DNA for genetic markers and evaluating your lifestyle with factors such as sex, age, personal practices and current health status that may influence your risks associated to COVID-19.

The novel coronavirus has spread rapidly throughout the world. As a result, millions of people have been infected, yet we have not had much time to study it. This report is still in its alpha phase. As we research the relationship between genetics and COVID-19 further, this report will become more detailed and supported by more reliable studies.

The studies performed on this genetic trait are highly reliable. There is a strong statistical significance between these genetic markers and the results in your report.

Several studies were conducted on these traits and they are deemed to be statistically significant in terms of frequency, however, more research is needed for the existing results to be deemed conclusive.

These traits are based on suggestive research, wherein only small population sizes and descriptive statistics were used to prove the studies' hypotheses.

Signs & Symptoms

Once diagnosed with COVID-19, patients exhibited varying symptoms, ranging from being asymptomatic (no symptoms) to severe or deadly reactions. Recent reports say that symptoms may appear from 2 to 14 days after exposure. Common symptoms may include:

  • Fever or chills
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Cough
  • Diarrhea
  • Sore throat
