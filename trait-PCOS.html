Risk Management & Tests

Risks for PCOS involve an interaction between genetics and lifestyle. Since it is a complex disorder, there is still a chance for you to develop it, even after ruling out your genetic risks. Although, you can definitely manage the varying symptoms and decrease the effects of it, in case you acquire it. You can also reduce the risk of secondary concerns through your lifestyle choices.

What You Can Do:
  • Maintain a healthy weight through exercise. Losing weight can reduce insulin and androgen levels and may restore ovulation.
  • Limit carbohydrate intake. This will help keep balanced insulin levels and prevent obesity.
  • Avoid processed foods. These can exacerbate your symptoms, especially acne.

Impaired fertility due to PCOS can be managed through medications that induce ovulation. But non-pharmacologic management should still also be followed to further increase the chances of conceiving.
